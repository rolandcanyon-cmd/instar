## 1. Problem statement — the incident (evidence-grounded)

2026-07-10, times UTC. The Laptop — owner of topics 29723 (live apprenticeship autonomous run) and 29836 (postmortem, hard-pinned to Laptop) — crash-looped ~23:15–23:49 (bloated 50MB replicated journal × new boot-time scan × supervisor kill cadence; fixed separately). Inbound messages for both topics arrived at the Mac Mini (lease holder / front door):

1. The Mini's router produced the correct verdict: `[session-pool] route topic 29723 → action=queued owner=? self=m_4cbc… acked=false`, and for 29836 `[session-router] attention: No machine available for session — 29836: hard-pin-unsatisfiable`.
2. **6 ms later** the inbound handler's fall-through spawned locally anyway: `[telegram→session] Session "…" needs respawn` → `[SessionManager] Spawning interactive session…` → `[telegram] Registered topic 29723 <-> session…` (29836 at 23:19:51).
3. **7 s later** the Mini's one-voice gate logged `SpeakerElection topic 29723: speak=false (owner-other)` — one subsystem knew the topic belonged elsewhere seconds after another spawned for it.
4. The Laptop recovered 23:49:47 with its tmux-persistent sessions intact → live duplicates on both machines.
5. Nothing converged them: the duplicate detector is observe-only; the post-transfer closeout was structurally inert (§2.3); the Laptop's reaper skipped its copies (`skipped:not-lease-holder`), the Mini's skipped its own (`skipped:open-commitment`).
6. Manual heal 00:43:30Z (remote-close, audited in `logs/remote-close-audit.jsonl`).

Dormant same-class hazard found during the investigation: a frozen Mini resume-queue entry ("revive 29836 HERE", queued Jul 5 while the queue was paused) — cancelled by hand 2026-07-11. (§2.4 explains why the drain's own guards would *probably* have invalidated it, and why "probably" isn't good enough.)

**Root cause, one sentence:** the routing verdict is computed and then discarded — the inbound handler's short-circuit keys on durable-queue custody (`acked`) rather than on the ownership verdict, the durable queue that would set `acked` ships dark, and the router's own `handleLocally` is a no-op — so a non-owner front door falls through to the legacy local spawn, and no reconciler converges the resulting duplicate.

## 3. Proposed design

Four deterministic layers (A–D) + two LLM judgment points (J1, J2) inside them. **Consistency model, stated once:** ownership-record writes are single-writer-serialized — normal placement writes go through each machine's local CAS + journal replication; RECONCILIATION writes (repairs) are made only by the serving-lease holder, carry the lease epoch as a fencing token plus the record's monotonic `ownershipEpoch`, and a peer applies a repair only if the fencing epoch is current and the record epoch advances. Two machines cannot both be reconciler (fenced lease); two machines CAN both believe they own a topic (async replication) — that state is a first-class reconciler input (§3.2.2), resolved on epochs, never wall-clock recency. **Epoch hygiene:** at boot, a machine re-seeds its epoch high-water from the REPLICATED (peer-merged) journal before participating in placement — a backup-restored machine cannot mint below-water epochs (round-2); and an epoch row is admissible as evidence only if its first-hop origin-stamp validates (the CommitmentsSync forged-row precedent) — a peer cannot attribute epochs to a third machine.

**Glossary (implementer-facing):** *floor* = the deterministic bound on an arbiter's action space; *rung* = a step of the owner-dark ladder (§3.3); *dark* = shipped but disabled by config; *dryRun* = enabled but observe-only; *G1 path* = the deterministic cold-start notice sender (`telegram.sendToTopic`, no LLM gate); *F8 carve-out* = the existing lease-gate bypass for closeout terminations; *fenced lease* = the pool's clock-proof, epoch-numbered serving-lease (one machine "in charge" at a time); *journal replication* = the append-only replicated event log machines use to materialize each other's state; *WS4.4(f) pool poll-cache* = the shared per-peer poll cache that feeds pool-scope views; *U4.1 HLC fold* = the pin store's hybrid-logical-clock merge with skew-quarantine; *episode* = one contiguous occurrence of a condition (an owner-dark outage, a registry-error streak, a duplicate's existence) with a defined open/close. **A duplicate episode OPENS at the contested copy's spawn-registration timestamp** — backdated at detection, so evidence minted after the bootleg spawn can never predate the episode (§3.4).

### 3.0 Consistency contract (guarantees and non-goals — stated so nobody infers consensus)
This design deliberately uses instar's EXISTING coordination substrate (fenced serving-lease, per-machine CAS registry, journal replication) rather than an external consensus system (§3.7). The contract, explicitly:
- **Safety guarantees:** reconciliation writes are single-writer (fenced lease + record epoch — a stale reconciler's writes are rejected; ownership records only — pin repairs ride the pin store's own fold, §3.2.3); the closeout never fires without its own confirm-ticks + fresh guard re-checks; **no auto-close without terminate-time owner-liveness proof — the re-probe is a precondition of EVERY `duplicate-reconciled` and `topic-moved` closeout termination at the shared chokepoint** (not only the carve-out legs; round-4); custody transfer is ACK-gated with no silent drop path.
- **Liveness guarantees (best-effort, bounded):** a detectable duplicate converges within a small number of reconciler ticks once the involved machines are mutually reachable and the substrate is ready; user silence during an owner-dark episode is bounded by the 10-min ceiling (notice, if not answer).
- **Explicit NON-goals:** ownership is NOT linearizable; there is NO guaranteed single owner during partitions, replication lag, or registry-error episodes — the one-owner invariant is *enforced on the resolvable path and CONVERGED TOWARD under faults*, not absolute; the `error`-arm fail-open can transiently create a duplicate (and duplicated external side effects are not reversible) — the §0 argued exemption, chosen because silent unreachability is the worse failure. §5 carries a partition/delayed-replay test matrix row exercising exactly these windows.

### 3.1 Layer A — Binding verdict at the spawn seam (prevention)
**A new `SpawnAdmission` check, consulted at every session-creating callsite for a conversation-bound topic** (Telegram cold-spawn `:2691`, Telegram respawn `:2613/:2631`, Slack `:9048/:9358`; the drain tail and revival actors already gate). Mechanics:
1. `resolveOwnershipSafe(sessionKey)` — a non-throwing tri-state wrapper over the ownership registry returning `{kind: 'self' | 'other-alive' | 'other-dark' | 'unowned' | 'error'}` (closes the §2.1 ambiguity; `other-dark` derives from the existing `isMachineAlive` input; `error` catches registry read failures instead of leaking a throw into the handler's fail-open catch). **It reads the in-memory/cached registry view only — never a synchronous durable read on the inbound path.**
2. **One resolution per message (TOCTOU guard):** when the router already produced a verdict for this message id, the seam CONSUMES that verdict rather than re-resolving — the admission decision and the routing decision cannot disagree mid-dispatch.
3. Admission table (deterministic floor; rows lettered for reference):
   - (a) `self` → spawn (today's behavior).
   - (b) `other-alive` → forward (existing owner-side inject; today's behavior when routing works).
   - (c) `other-dark` → **never spawn locally.** Run the owner-dark ladder (§3.3). *(Enforced only where durable custody exists — otherwise dryRun; item 6.)*
   - (d) `unowned` → route through `placeAndClaim` and spawn ONLY as the claimed owner (the router already does this; the seam makes its result binding).
   - (e) `error` → spawn locally (reachability wins over a broken store), **bounded:** once per topic per error-episode, one journal row (`logs/owner-dark-ladder.jsonl`) + ONE deduped attention item (dedupe key `spawn-admission-error:<machineId>:<episode>`, wording in FD11). **Error-arm breaker (flap-proof, round-2):** trips on K=5 consecutive errors OR a windowed rate of ≥8 errors in 10 min regardless of interleaved successes; an episode closes only after J=10 consecutive clean resolutions (hysteresis); ≥3 episodes per machine per 24h escalates the item to HIGH. Breaker-open degrades arm (e) to the rung-3 notice floor until the episode closes. **These breaker bounds are CODE CONSTANTS, not config** (safety bounds — the self-action-backpressure precedent), tunable only by PR. During a registry-error episode the reconciler and closeout FREEZE (same fault domain — they read the same store), so the error arm cannot be exploited to both mint and "heal" duplicates; duplicates formed mid-episode converge after recovery (the Cross-Machine Coherence argued exemption, §0).
4. The `queued`/`placement-blocked` verdicts suppress local spawn **independently of `acked`**: if the durable queue is dark, the fallback is the deterministic G1-style notice path (§3.3 rung 3), not the legacy spawn. *(Enforced only where durable custody exists — otherwise dryRun; item 6.)*
5. Single-machine installs / pool `dark`: the seam short-circuits to `spawn` — byte-identical behavior, zero regression.
6. **Restated as an admission-table invariant (round-4, codex):** with the inbound queue dark, `ownershipGatedSpawn` CANNOT block a spawn outside dryRun — rows (c) and the `queued` suppression are enforceable only where durable custody exists; an implementer can never wire notice-only refusal on the fleet by accident.
Rollout flag: `multiMachine.sessionPool.ownershipGatedSpawn` (shape + semantics in §4). **Enforcement precondition (round-2, codex):** Layer A ENFORCES only on a pool whose inbound queue is live (rung-3 durable custody exists there); everywhere else it runs dryRun. The §3.3 matrix's "fleet today" row describes the DRYRUN would-be path, not an enforceable state — refusing to spawn with nothing but a resend notice would trade reachability for coherence, and the honest trade (§3.7) only clears once custody exists.

### 3.2 Layer B — Duplicate reconciler = converge the ownership record (auto-heal)

#### 3.2.0 Substrate precondition
Layer B's enforcement flip is **gated on the durable + replicated ownership substrate being live on every pool machine** (`LocalSessionOwnershipStore` + journal materialization, pool-consistent activation — the existing `server.ts:18952-18963` signal pattern). On the fleet default (in-memory store, no replication) `other-alive`/`other-dark` are unresolvable cross-machine and convergence writes are meaningless; the reconciler refuses to arm there (a loud `substrate-not-ready` status, never silent). **Substrate flap:** readiness is re-evaluated per tick; a NOT-ready tick pauses the reconciler (status + one deduped item if it persists >30 min), while the seam keeps running on its local cached view — replication degradation degrades seam verdicts toward the `unowned`/`error` arms, both bounded-loud. All three pool-behavior flags — `ownershipGatedSpawn`, `duplicateReconciler`, AND `commitmentCustodyTransfer` — join the machine-coherence guard's coherence-critical set (`COHERENCE_CRITICAL_FLAGS`, `src/core/machineCoherenceManifest.ts:93`; custody skew degrades escalate-safe, but a split pool should still be visible) — noting honestly that the guard itself is dev-gated dryRun-first, so **pool-consistent activation is the PRIMARY defense** against flag skew; the guard is the alarm layer where it runs.

#### 3.2.1 Trigger, cadence, and input freshness
1. Runs on the serving-lease holder as its own tick (`reconcilerTickMs`, default 60s) **riding the WS4.4(f) shared pool poll-cache** for candidate discovery when that cache is live; where the cache is dark (it is dev-gated), the declared fallback is a lease-holder-only bounded direct fan-out at tick cadence (N−1 peer reads per 60s — acceptable). A candidate row from cache/`stale:true` is never acted on: before any record write the reconciler makes a **fresh direct probe** (5s budget per probe; probe failure/timeout → defer the topic, counts toward the episode's 3 attempts) to the involved machines confirming (a) the duplicate still exists and (b) the intended owner holds a LIVE copy (`target-has-live-copy` precondition).
2. Per-tick caps: `maxReconcilesPerTick` (default 3) and `maxConvergenceWritesPerTick` (default 5).
3. The reconciler DEFERS a topic while it has: an in-flight transfer, an open stale-owner-release episode, or an active owner-dark hold (§3.3) — one authority in motion per topic at a time.
4. **Partition honesty:** the lease holder can only heal what it can observe. Duplicates wholly inside a partition it cannot reach persist until the partition heals — accepted; the mesh's existing partition alarms (rope-health `urgent`) cover the visibility gap.

#### 3.2.2 Intended-owner determination (evidence-ordered)
Deterministic rules, in order — each rule requires its evidence class, and **peer-poll self-reports are never sufficient on their own**:
1. **Hard pin** (pin store; a quarantined pin never counts).
2. **Highest `ownershipEpoch`** among ADMISSIBLE records (origin-stamp-validated, §3 epoch hygiene) in any machine's replicated registry view — NEVER wall-clock recency. Symmetric divergence — both registries claim `self` — is a first-class input resolved here: higher admissible epoch wins; equal → rule 3.
3. **Server-registered live autonomous run** for the topic — read from the journal-replicated run-registration snapshot (server-minted at `POST /autonomous/register`), confirmed by an authenticated live probe to the hosting machine; never a bare peer poll row.
   **Rule-2/rule-3 contradiction guard (round-2):** if the epoch winner ≠ the machine hosting a server-registered live run, that is an ESCALATE, never a converge — the epoch may be stale-but-higher (backup restore, replication lag) and live work evidence must not be overridden mechanically.
4. Otherwise: **judgment point J2** (§3.4) with its mechanical-corroboration floor; J2 unavailable or evidence-free → **escalate ONE attention item** (never guess silently). "Most recent user interaction" is deliberately NOT a rule — the non-owner duplicate often has the latest message BECAUSE of the bug; duplicate-side recency as the only differentiator is an escalate, not a signal.
**Both-copies-carry-live-runs:** always `escalate-to-attention`, no auto-converge — the "never close a run-carrier" and "owner survives" floors would contradict, and a reconciler-initiated transfer would meet the 409 `needsConfirmation` consent gate an autonomous actor must not answer. A reconciler action that receives 409 ESCALATES, never retries.

#### 3.2.3 Convergence mechanics
1. Repairs write through `SessionOwnershipRegistry.cas` (`src/core/SessionOwnershipRegistry.ts:182`) with the §3 fencing (lease epoch + record epoch). Pin-store coherence repairs go through the **pin store's own write path** (the U4.1 HLC fold; a repair colliding with pin skew-quarantine defers to the quarantine — readmit stays the operator's PIN-gated action). Write order is fixed: ownership record first, pin repair second; a crash between them is healed idempotently on the next tick.
2. **Peer-echo confirmation:** after a convergence write, the reconciler confirms — within `echoConfirmTicks` reconciler ticks (default 4; config, sized to measured journal-hop + applier latency: the peer's `OwnershipApplier` materializes every 15s, but the journal hop itself is the variable to measure during Increment-1 soak) — that EVERY non-owner machine's own registry view now names the converged owner (via the existing authenticated mesh read). No echo from a machine → escalate `convergence-not-observed` naming the unconfirmed machines. **Per-tick aggregation (P17):** all `convergence-not-observed` escalations in a tick fold into ONE attention item enumerating topics. **Lying/buggy echo backstop:** a machine that echoes the record but keeps its duplicate is re-detected as a persistent duplicate next tick; echoed-but-unhealed episodes count toward the §3.2.5 breaker, so the loop is bounded.
3. The existing post-transfer closeout sweeper then closes non-owner copies through its normal gated path (`topicMovedCloseout`, 2 confirm ticks, veto breaker, F8 lease carve-out) — reap-log reason extended to name `duplicate-reconciled`. **Terminate-time owner-liveness re-probe is a precondition of EVERY `duplicate-reconciled` and `topic-moved` termination at this chokepoint** (5s budget, fail/timeout → veto + defer to next tick) — not only the §3.2.4a carve-out legs; the reconciler's pre-write probe is ticks-to-minutes stale by terminate time, and the §3.0 contract's "no auto-close without terminate-time proof" must hold for unshielded copies too (round-4). **No new killer is introduced.**
4. A `duplicate-reconciled` close is **never resume-queue-eligible at enqueue time** (not merely invalidated at drain).

#### 3.2.4 Survivor floor (deterministic)
Owner's copy survives by default; a non-owner copy that is mid-turn / carrying live subagents defers via the existing work-gate probe (`SessionRefresh.consultWorkGate`, `src/core/SessionRefresh.ts:413-426` / `SessionManager.probeWorkState`); a non-owner copy driving a LIVE autonomous run is never closed — ownership MOVES to it via the deliberate transfer path instead (except both-run → escalate, §3.2.2); protected sessions never auto-closed. Unanswered-inbound recovery: when the closed copy held the topic's last unanswered message, recovery uses durable-queue custody where live; where the queue is dark, the honest floor is the rung-3 resend notice — `resolveUnansweredInbound` (`SessionRefresh.ts:196-198`) is LOCAL machinery and is used only when the survivor is on the same machine.

**3.2.4a The commitment-shield correction (round-2 redesign — six reviewers convergent):** a converged record alone leaves the incident's Mini-side copy immortal (`open-commitment` KEEP re-checked fresh at terminate). The guard is TOPIC-scoped (`activeCommitmentForTopic`), commitments are origin-locked records with no session field (§2.5/§2.6), and `CommitmentsSync` replicas are not driveable — so there is no "session re-bind." The correction has two parts:

- **A narrow closeout carve-out for the `open-commitment` and `recent-user-message` KEEP guards**, honored by the sweeper ONLY when BOTH hold **at terminate time on the closing machine**: (i) a provenance fence appropriate to the disposition is present in the local registry view — for `duplicate-reconciled`, the reconciler-minted, lease+epoch-fenced convergence record for this topic/episode; for `topic-moved`, the **transfer-minted, CAS+epoch-fenced ownership record the deliberate move itself wrote** (round-3: a move never produces a reconciler record, so requiring one would make the mirror leg unsatisfiable; a reap-reason string alone NEVER satisfies either leg — the forgery-rejection test covers both); and (ii) a **terminate-time re-probe** confirms the owner's copy is live and serving (mirroring how `attemptCloseoutTerminate` re-checks every guard fresh, and how the F8 precedent gates same-tick — `SessionManager.ts:1270-1294`); the re-probe inherits the 5s budget and must not block the sweeper's other decisions; probe fail/timeout → veto (KEEP), defer to next tick. Every other KEEP guard (active-subagent, structural-long-work, in-flight, protected) still vetoes — those shield real work, not topic bookkeeping.
- **Post-close commitment custody transfer (record-level, origin-stamp-preserving; its own sub-feature, round-3):** after a `duplicate-reconciled` (or `topic-moved`, below) close SUCCEEDS, the reconciler drives custody: it reads the closed copy's machine's open topic-scoped commitments (existing authenticated mesh read), the OWNER machine mints successor commitments through its own existing `POST /commitments` API with idempotent `externalKey` = `custody:<topicId>:<origin-commitment-id>` (the successor carries the origin's `userRequest`/`agentResponse`/`type`/`topicId` and a `custodyOf` cross-ref; peer-derived free text is **type/length-clamped and envelope-treated on receive** like every replicated-store ingest; the mint respects the owner's `maxActiveBeacons` activation cap), and on ACK the ORIGIN machine mutates its own records (its own single-writer CAS, via a dedicated origin-side supersede verb — §8) to a terminal `superseded-by-ownership-move` state — audited, beacon-terminal, never counted as violated (`sessionEpoch`'s `session-lost` check is disarmed by the terminal state; `supersededBy` precedent, `CommitmentTracker.ts:121`). **The supersede is fenced to a VERIFIED successor:** it requires the successor's externalKey + owner machineId and verifies successor existence via the authenticated mesh read before the CAS — no bare Bearer caller can terminal-supersede a live commitment. No ACK → the origin records stay untouched and ONE `custody-transfer-failed` item escalates (the promise is never silently dropped; `PromiseBeacon`'s existing owner-other self-suppression covers the gap window without duplicate beaconing). A VETOED close transfers nothing — custody moves only after a confirmed close, so there is no rollback path to get wrong. **Rollout boundary:** custody transfer ships behind its own sub-flag `multiMachine.sessionPool.commitmentCustodyTransfer` (independently reversible, Increment 2b, its own two-node E2E); with it disabled or unavailable, an open-commitment duplicate is NOT auto-closed — it escalates (`custody-transfer-unavailable`), the safe degradation.
- **Mirror parity (round-2, lessons-aware):** the sibling `topic-moved` disposition flows through the SAME sweeper with the SAME gap — a deliberately-moved topic with an open commitment leaves an immortal old copy today. The carve-out + custody transfer apply to BOTH dispositions (one gate set, one mirror — PR #1125's lesson applied at the shared chokepoint), and transfer-traceable episodes (reap-log reason `topic-moved`) are EXCLUDED from the §3.2.5 re-duplication breaker so operator move patterns cannot clamp the ladder.

#### 3.2.5 P19 breaker
A topic re-duplicating ≥3 times in 24h stops being auto-reconciled and raises ONE attention item. Counters are **per-topic episode counts in the replicated store**, receive-side type-clamped like every replicated store (finite bounded numbers, ISO-8601 timestamps; reject otherwise — an unclamped peer row must not clamp a conversation's ladder), TTL-pruned when idle >24h; counter rows ride the replication journal's post-fix size bounding (the 50MB-bloat fix — declared, not assumed). Record FLIPS and echoed-but-unhealed episodes count toward the breaker; transfer-traceable episodes do not clamp — but ≥3 transfer-traceable episodes per topic per 24h raise ONE observability-only attention item (round-3: a flapping automated mover must not be invisible just because it is exempt from the clamp; the transfer path's own 409 rate limit is the brake). **Breaker-open also clamps the owner-dark ladder for that topic to rung 3** (no claim-then-spawn), so the duplicate GENERATOR is braked together with the healer.

#### 3.2.6 Layer D fallout (corrected)
Because the reconciler converges the RECORD and §3.2.4a corrects the two topic-bookkeeping KEEP guards at the shared closeout chokepoint, the `not-lease-holder` and `open-commitment` shields stop making duplicates immortal — through the closeout's own gated path, not a new reap authority. A duplicate additionally shielded by REAL work guards (active-subagent, structural-long-work) ends at the closeout breaker's attention item — the honest design intent, stated: work-guarded duplicates escalate to the operator rather than being auto-killed.

#### 3.2.7 Supervision tier (P7)
The reconciler is **tier 0 declared-with-justification**: every non-mechanical choice routes to J2 or to the attention queue; the mechanical remainder (epoch comparison, CAS write, echo check, custody transfer) is deterministic with structural brakes. The graded-review job (§3.5) is tier 1.

### 3.3 The owner-dark ladder (Layer A's `other-dark` arm)

**Rollout-stage behavior matrix — hold-policy liveness is its OWN dimension (round-2: `holdForStability` trails the queue by one stage by design):**

| Stage | Rung 1 (hold) | Rung 2 (claim) | Rung 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fleet today (queue dark, hold n/a, release dry) | none | none (would-claim logged) | *descriptive only — Layer A is dryRun here (§3.1); the would-be path is the resend notice* |
| Dev pool: queue live, hold still trailing (dry), release dry | none — rung 1 skipped honestly | logged would-claim | durable-queue custody + "saved, will be answered" notice |
| Dev pool: queue live, hold live, release dry | 90s hold (`holdMaxMs`) | logged would-claim | durable-queue custody + notice |
| Dev pool: queue live, hold live, release live | 90s hold | real fenced claim via the single CAS funnel | durable-queue custody + notice |

1. **Hold** — the existing hold-for-stability shape (`holdVerdict`, `src/commands/server.ts:21258-21268`; `holdMaxMs:90000`, `src/core/inboundQueueConfig.ts:77-79`). Holds are capped: `maxConcurrentHolds` (default 20 per machine); over-cap → rung 3 immediately. Hold state is in-memory in-flight delivery state; a crash mid-hold loses at most the held messages (≤ cap), floored by the resend-wording notice; durable-queue custody removes the window where live.
2. **Claim-then-spawn** — if the owner is provably dead per the stale-owner-release evidence bar, the claim goes through the existing single CAS funnel (`StaleOwnerReleaseEngine.actForceClaim` → `reconciler.actStaleOwnerForceClaim`, `src/commands/server.ts:19339`); the new owner then spawns as `self`. **Dependency posture (G3):** this rung is inert while stale-owner-release is dryRun; the engine gains `loadBearing:true` + `criticalPath:'owner-dark-ladder'` on `/guards` when `ownershipGatedSpawn` enforces, and Layer A's enforcement on a pool is sequenced NO EARLIER than stale-owner-release leaving dryRun on that pool (§4).
3. **Queue + honest notice** — death unprovable (wobbly-but-alive): durable-queue custody when the inbound queue is live; when it is dark, the deterministic floor is **notice-only, with state-accurate wording** (FD9): dark-queue *"That conversation's machine is temporarily unreachable (it may be restarting). I can't safely answer from here, and I'm not holding your message — please resend in a few minutes, or send it to my Lifeline topic if it's urgent."*; queue-live *"…your message is saved and will be answered automatically when it returns."* Delivered on the **deterministic G1 path** (`telegram.sendToTopic` — the cold-start-lifeline precedent), **bypassing speaker election with a declared owner-dark exception** (election rule 1 is owner-liveness-blind, §2.6, and would structurally silence the notice). Guards on the exception: a **final owner-liveness re-check immediately before the send** (shrinks the contradictory-voices window to the probe RTT — a just-recovered owner answering normally suppresses the notice), plus a **topic-scoped suppression check** (recent identical notice visible in the topic's send history — episode dedupe is machine-scoped, so under split-brain two front doors could otherwise each notice; round-2). Dedupe: ONE notice per (topic, outage-episode), per-topic cooldown 30 min (`noticeCooldownMs`). Owner recovery closes the episode.

**Silence ceiling:** `ownerDarkLadder.maxUserSilenceMs` default **600000 (10 min)** — measured from the episode's first held/refused message to a real answer or the rung-3 notice; J1 may shorten but never extend past it. (Calibration note: with hold dry, the path is refuse→notice inside the first minute; with hold live, 90s hold precedes the choice — either way the ceiling binds.)

**J1 invocation discipline:** J1 runs per **(owner-machine, outage-episode)** — ONE arbiter call when the episode opens, cached verdict applied to every topic/message in that episode, re-invoked only on state change: evidence bar newly passed, episode crossing the ceiling, or an URGENCY-CLASS change in arriving messages (without this trigger, urgency would be inert after the first message — round-2; re-invocations are capped at 3 per episode — urgency oscillation is content-drivable spend, round-3 — excess collapses to the cached verdict). TTL'd at the ceiling. Timeout budget 5s; timeout/shed → deterministic default (fixed hold → notice at ceiling). Arbiter calls ride `buildIntelligenceProvider` as **non-gating** traffic.

### 3.4 The judgment points (first live instances of Judgment Within Floors)
Shared contract (this IS the new standard, applied):
- **Bounded action space** — the model picks among options the floor proved safe; invalid/omitted/timeout → deterministic default. The floor can only be narrowed, never widened.
- **Bench-laddered fallback** — the arbiter model resolves via the routing registry's per-task record `{default, fallbacks[], floor}`; below-floor or all-doors-down → the deterministic default. The last rung is always static.
- **Never on the happy path** — invoked only on the ambiguous branch. Zero added latency otherwise.
- **Untrusted-data envelopes on ALL free text, both arbiters** — message-derived urgency, journal prose, and session tails are delimited untrusted data; the provenance row records the enveloped form. Envelopes bound instruction-following, NOT evidence-weighting — which is why the floors require mechanical corroboration wherever a poisoned tail could steer a floor-legal wrong outcome.
- **Provenance-logged** (§3.5), attributed, metered, benched.

**J1 — owner-dark rung arbiter** (`attribution.component: 'OwnerDarkArbiter'`). Context: crash-loop signature, heartbeat/git-sync recency, rope health, outage history, queue depth, message urgency (enveloped). Action space: `{keep-holding≤ceiling, queue+notify-now, proceed-to-claim-evaluation}`. J1 proposes; the claim itself still passes the mechanical evidence bar — J1 can NEVER authorize a claim.

**J2 — survivor arbiter** (`attribution.component: 'DuplicateSurvivorArbiter'`). Context: both copies' recent tmux tails (fetched via the live-tail chokepoint, **redacted on the SERVING machine before the wire**, byte-capped at the chokepoint's existing bound, quoted as untrusted data), autonomous-run state location, open commitments, session ages, last user interaction. Action space: `{owner-copy-survives, move-ownership-to-working-copy, defer-one-tick, escalate-to-attention}`. Floors:
- `move-ownership-to-working-copy` is a PROPOSAL, valid only with **mechanical corroboration that PREDATES the duplicate episode's open** — a server-registered autonomous run or commitment whose registration/creation timestamp precedes episode-open, where **episode-open = the contested copy's spawn-registration timestamp** (backdated at detection; round-3 — a detection-time reading would let post-spawn evidence "predate" a late-detected episode). The live work-gate probe corroborates ONLY `defer-one-tick` / close-veto — never a move (the probe is post-episode evidence by construction: the contested copy answering the very misrouted message that created it passes any liveness probe). Evidence the contested copy minted after its own bootleg spawn never corroborates a move.
- `defer-one-tick` is capped at 3 consecutive; the 4th tick forces **`escalate-to-attention`** (three defers are evidence of ambiguity, not a license to close — round-2 pinned the terminal).
- All §3.2.4 floor guards apply regardless of choice.
- Escalation bodies (J2's `escalate-to-attention`, `convergence-not-observed`, both-run) are **fixed templates + metadata** (topic, machines, reason enum) — never arbiter- or tail-derived free text (an injection relay into the operator's rendered context otherwise).

**Registry obligations (mechanical, each CI-ratcheted):** each arbiter joins `COMPONENT_CATEGORY` (`src/core/componentCategories.ts:36`) as category **`sentinel`**, task nature **B** in `natureRoutingMap.ts`, `LLM_UNTRUSTED_INPUT: true` with **injection-exposed** classification per the injection-exposure map in `src/core/natureRoutingMap.ts:48,84` (gates injection-unsafe doors), `LLM_BENCH_COVERAGE` (`src/data/llmBenchCoverage.ts:32`) with parity-checked batteries `research/llm-pathway-bench/instar-bench-v2/tasks/{owner-dark-arbiter,duplicate-survivor}.json` (`source` anchor + `promptFidelity: verbatim`, cases across the five stress axes, seeded from the 2026-07-10 and 2026-07-05 incidents), a row in `docs/LLM-ROUTING-REGISTRY.md` (freshness lint), and a **bench-ladder floor tier of `capable`**. Precedent: `ExternalHogClassifier`.

### 3.5 Decision provenance & outcome review (net-new capability)
Verified gap: no durable full-context log exists today — `ResponseReviewDecisionLog` deliberately caps at 200 scrubbed chars + `contextMeta` (`src/core/CoherenceGate.ts:1552-1580`). This layer adds one:
- **`JudgmentProvenanceLog`** (JSONL + rotation via SafeFsExecutor, per deciding machine): one row per arbiter call — full context snapshot as handed to the model (enveloped form), options presented, decision + stated reason, floor bounds in force, model/door, tokens, latency, fallback rung. **Scope honesty (round-2, codex):** the log never contains MORE than the model saw — and what the model sees is already the bounded, chokepoint-redacted form (tails redacted+byte-capped at the live-tail chokepoint before the wire); "full context" means full FIDELITY to the model's input, not raw system state.
- **Storage + exposure:** rows live under `state/judgment-provenance/` — directory mode 0700, files 0600, **gitignored** (delivered to FRESH installs via `ensureGitignore` AND to existing agents via an idempotent `PostUpdateMigrator` gitignore patch, the `.worktrees/` precedent at `PostUpdateMigrator.ts:2532-2549` — round-3: agent homes are git repos and `state/` is untracked-but-not-ignored today, so without the migration a broad `git add` would commit provenance rows cross-machine), backup/support-bundle EXCLUDED. HTTP exposure is closed by a **NET-NEW deny mechanism**: `NEVER_SERVED_PREFIXES`, a hardcoded (config-immune) prefix list enforced inside the file-routes path validator against the **fully-resolved (realpath) project-relative path** (a symlink inside an allowed path cannot evade it — round-3), covering list/read/download/link AND the WS4.4 pool-link peer-proxy path, and **implying edit-deny at the same chokepoints** (a never-served path is never-editable — otherwise a confused session could poison provenance rows that the graded-review job replays into bench batteries). (§2.6: no such mechanism exists today — `NEVER_EDITABLE_PREFIXES` blocks edits only, and `blockedFilenames` is config-mutable; this is a named build, not a precedent join. Wiring tests: GET/download/edit on `state/judgment-provenance/*` → 403 with default config, including a symlink-evasion case and the proxy path.) **Threat model, explicit (round-5):** the at-rest protections defend against HTTP exposure, accidental git/backup propagation, and casual filesystem browsing; an attacker with root/user-level access to the machine's disk is OUT OF SCOPE — the same posture as every sibling machine-local store (relationships, logs, operationalFacts, per the at-rest-honesty standard). **Redaction honesty:** `scrubString` + `liveTailRedaction` are credential-shape scrubbers, NOT PII scrubbing — hence machine-local, deny-listed, short-retention. The `FailureDetail {redacted, full}` split (`src/monitoring/FailureLedger.ts:74`) governs the HTTP surface: `GET /judgment-provenance` (Bearer) serves REDACTED rows only, redacts on the serving machine before the wire, `?scope=pool` merges type/length-clamped peer rows as untrusted data. **The redaction contract is an INVARIANT, not config**; retention IS config (default **14 days** — round-3 externals: shortest window that still feeds the graded-review cadence). Encrypt-at-rest was considered and not adopted: the posture (0700/0600, deny-listed, gitignored, backup-excluded, short retention) matches every other machine-local log, and the vault's encryption is not streamable for append-heavy JSONL — documented trade, not an oversight.
- **Write discipline:** async buffered appends only; per-row byte clamp 64KB; `provenance.deterministicSampling` (default 1.0 during Increment-1 soak; **step-down criterion pinned:** Increment-2 entry drops the default to 0.1) — arbiter rows are always written.
- **Outcome annotation:** ground truth appended when it arrives — owner-return timestamps, reconciler results, resend/complaint signals.
- **Graded review:** a cadenced, budget-capped, tier-1-supervised job (ships OFF; maturation path: registered `DARK_GATE_EXCLUSIONS` cost-bearing entry — budget-capped LLM replay — with the dev-agent-enabled soak → fleet-criteria ladder) replays sampled rows with outcomes attached and grades correct / wrong-but-reasonable / wrong; verdicts route to needs-stronger-model vs needs-better-prompt vs needs-better-context; graded real cases feed the battery. Review NEVER regrades floors. Output: ONE digest attention item, operator-review-gated.

### 3.7 Alternatives considered (round-1 + round-2 external reviewers)
- **etcd/ZooKeeper-class lock manager or Kafka-class queue:** rejected — instar's design decision #1 is file-based state with zero external infra dependencies; the mesh already carries a fenced serving lease and a replicated journal. The gap the incident exposed was not a missing primitive but an UNENFORCED verdict.
- **Deterministic tie-breaker instead of J2 (lowest machine id / earliest session):** the deterministic ladder IS complete without J2 — rule 4's static fallback is escalate-to-attention, and Increment 2 runs exactly that way. J2 exists because the incident-class residue is precisely where static rules were the proven failure; an arbitrary tie-break picks a winner with zero evidence. J2's authority is bounded to proposals over pre-episode mechanical evidence.
- **Pure workflow/state-machine policy for owner-dark timing (no J1):** viable — and it is the deterministic default the floor ships with. J1 is an optimization within it, justified by the bench flywheel. If the graded review shows J1 ≤ static default, J1 is removed — the standard's own outcome-review teeth.
- **Operator-escalation-forever for commitment-shielded duplicates (round-5, codex):** viable and it IS the shipped behavior until Increment 2b (escalate-not-close). Custody transfer exists because the incident class recurs unattended (overnight, 2×in one week) and an escalation queue that only a human can drain re-creates the manual-heal status quo for the most common shield; the 2b flip is justified by the frequency measured during Increment 2's escalate-only soak — if commitment-shielded duplicates turn out rare, 2b simply never flips.
- **Topic-owned commitment execution instead of record transfer (round-4, codex):** re-keying commitment EXECUTION to the topic's current owner (beacons fire wherever the topic lives, records stay put) was considered — it would eliminate the transfer verb but requires re-keying the whole commitment lifecycle (PromiseBeacon, delivery, CommitmentsSync are origin-machine-keyed today, §2.6) and makes every beacon tick a cross-machine ownership lookup on the happy path. Record transfer at the rare reconcile/move boundary is the minimal change preserving the origin-locked single-writer model; revisit if custody transfers turn out to be frequent.
- **Ops-cost comparison for the consensus rejection (round-4, codex):** an external DLM/queue adds a quorum service to operate (3+ nodes for meaningful availability — more machines than most instar pools), a new failure domain (the coordinator down = no placement at all vs today's degraded-but-reachable), a bootstrap circularity (agents must run on a bare laptop before any infra exists), and does not remove the hard part (the SEAM enforcement this spec adds). The existing substrate's failure cases are the §3.0 non-goals — disclosed and bounded rather than displaced onto a new dependency.
- **Minimal staged alternative (round-2, codex — "ship the deterministic gate first"):** that IS this plan. Increments 1–2 ship exactly the minimal deterministic fix (seam + reconciler, static defaults, no arbiters); J1/J2 arrive shadow-first in Increment 3 and acting in 4, each removable on graded-review evidence. The standards ride the same cycle because the operator explicitly directed Part-B ratification (§0), and the ratified pattern is what makes the arbiter increments reviewable at all.
- **The honest user-visible trade (round-2, adversarial):** today a wobbly-owner message gets an INSTANT answer from a fresh wrong-machine session — which splits the conversation's context across machines and mints an immortal duplicate (the incident). Under enforcement it gets custody-or-notice. One resend beats a context-less answer plus a duplicate; this is stated here so the fleet flip survives the operator's inevitable "why did my agent stop answering instantly?" — and it is exactly why enforcement WAITS for durable-queue custody (§3.1) rather than shipping notice-only.

## 7. Standards registry text (three `###` articles)

**Family: Building — `### Judgment Within Floors`**
**Rule.** A decision point with competing signals or non-enumerable context may be delegated to an LLM arbiter only inside a deterministic floor: the floor defines the complete safe action space and a conservative default; invariants are never delegated; the arbiter can narrow but never widen; an arbiter choice with irreversible consequence requires mechanical corroboration, never free-text evidence alone; fallback follows the bench-ranked ladder and always ends at a deterministic rung; **and an arbiter may begin ACTING (beyond shadow) only after shadow-phase evidence shows it beats the deterministic default on the decision point's named success criteria** (round-5 sharpening — evidence before authority). A new static heuristic at such a point must state why it is not a judgment point.
**Derives from.** *Signal vs. Authority* and *The Body and the Mind* — and sharpens rather than inverts them: floors are the documented exemption class (`docs/signal-vs-authority.md` §"When this principle does NOT apply" — enumerable-domain invariants and safety guards on irreversible actions) where deterministic blocking is sanctioned; ABOVE the floor the mind holds the choice, exactly as The Body and the Mind requires. Coexists with *Tiered Development*'s audited below-floor authority: a JWF floor is a per-decision-point action-space bound, not a tier gate; Tiered Development governs who may change the floor, JWF governs what runs inside it.
**In practice.** Applied through `src/core/SpawnAdmission.ts` (J1 floor) and the duplicate reconciler survivor floor; contested per-spec via the spec-converge decision-point classification and per-change via the side-effects question; arbiters join the four routing registries and carry parity-checked batteries.
**Earned from.** 2026-07-10 duplicate-session incident: the static reap heuristics (`open-commitment`, `not-lease-holder`) made duplicates immortal, while the missing enforcement of an already-computed verdict caused them.
**Traces to the goal.** The mind decides within the body's constraints; neither substitutes for the other.

**Family: Building — `### Decision Provenance & Outcome Review`**
**Rule.** Every LLM judgment call durably logs the full context it was handed and the decision it made — scrubbed, retention-bounded, machine-local-full/HTTP-redacted — and every judgment point is outcome-annotated where ground truth exists and periodically graded against outcomes, with graded real cases feeding its bench battery. An unlogged judgment call is an unaccountable one.
**Derives from.** *Observable Intelligence* — and extends it from call METADATA to decision CONTENT (the handed context, the choice, the outcome). A separate article because the obligations differ in kind: metadata is cheap and always-on; content carries disclosure risk and therefore carries the redaction/locality/retention contract as part of the rule itself.
**In practice.** Applied through `src/core/JudgmentProvenanceLog.ts` and the graded-review job; extends **Token-Audit Completeness** from cost to content.
**Earned from.** The 2026-07-10 investigation reconstructed decisions from scattered logs by hand; provenance rows would have made the root cause a read, and real incidents are the only honest battery cases.
**Traces to the goal.** Observable Intelligence — no autonomous decision is invisible, and the system's judgment measurably improves.

**Family: The Substrate — `### Ownership-Gated Side Effects`**
**Rule.** On a multi-machine pool, any actor that creates, revives, or re-binds a session — or fires topic-scoped side effects — must prove current conversation ownership at fire time; routing and ownership verdicts are binding, not advisory; a non-owner forwards, queues, or claims deliberately, never acts locally; ownership-resolution error fails toward action loudly and boundedly, never silently.
**In practice.** Applied through `src/core/SpawnAdmission.ts` and the burst-invariant E2E test; the revival actors' existing `topic-owner-elsewhere` invalidation is the precedent generalized. The §3.2.4a carve-out's terminate-time re-probe is this rule applied to the CLOSE side.
**Earned from.** 2026-07-10: the Mini spawned sessions for Laptop-owned topics 6ms after its own router said not to; 2026-06-25: topic-28730 ownership-split stall.
**Traces to the goal.** One agent, many machines — exactly one voice and one owner per conversation.
